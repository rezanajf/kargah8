The Great Gatsby is a novel set during the Roaring Twenties, a period marked by wealth, glamour, and excess in the United States. Narrated by Nick Carraway, the story centers on Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire known for his extravagant parties. Beneath the glittering surface, however, lies loneliness, illusion, and emotional emptiness.

Gatsby is driven by his desire to reunite with Daisy Buchanan, the woman he has loved for years. He believes that money and determination can restore the past and fulfill his dream. As Nick observes Gatsby’s world, he begins to see the moral carelessness and selfishness hidden beneath the elegance of high society.

The novel explores themes such as the American Dream, social class, love, and corruption. Fitzgerald uses vivid imagery and symbolism to give the story emotional depth, especially through the famous green light. The Great Gatsby remains one of the most important and widely studied novels in American literature.
