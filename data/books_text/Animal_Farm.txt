Animal Farm begins with the animals on Manor Farm listening to Old Major, an elderly boar who shares his dream of a world without human control. He describes humans as exploiters who take the animals’ labor while giving little in return. His speech inspires the animals to imagine rebellion and a new society built on equality.

After Old Major’s death, the animals rise up against Mr. Jones and successfully take over the farm. They rename it Animal Farm and establish a new set of principles meant to ensure fairness and collective ownership. At first, the revolution appears hopeful, and the animals believe they are building a better future together.

As time passes, the pigs begin to assume leadership and gradually claim more power for themselves. Napoleon, in particular, becomes increasingly authoritarian, manipulating language, fear, and memory to control the other animals. The novel develops into a sharp allegory about corruption, political betrayal, and the collapse of revolutionary ideals.
