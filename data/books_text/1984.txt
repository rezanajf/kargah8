1984 is a dystopian novel set in a world where the government watches, controls, and manipulates every part of human life. The story follows Winston Smith, a man living under the oppressive rule of the Party, led by the symbolic figure of Big Brother. In this society, truth is not fixed, because those in power constantly rewrite history.

Winston works at the Ministry of Truth, where his job is to alter past records so that they match the Party’s present claims. As he becomes more aware of the system’s lies, he starts to question the world around him and longs for freedom. His private rebellion, including his relationship with Julia, becomes a dangerous challenge to the regime.

The novel explores themes such as totalitarianism, surveillance, censorship, and psychological control. Orwell shows how language and information can be used as tools of domination. Because of its warning about power and propaganda, 1984 remains deeply relevant today.
